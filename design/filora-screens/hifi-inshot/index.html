Taste mapping — why each choice reads as InShot-grade, not stock Material

The dark-first charcoal canvas with a single blue #2563EB accent and edge-to-edge media previews is the InShot instinct — dark, bold, media-forward — applied without garishness (one accent + a fixed category wheel = Simple Design Ltd.’s “one system applied ruthlessly,” ~2B installs). Large real-looking thumbnails with rounded 16px corners, size/duration badges and play glyphs answer the “content is the hero” principle and directly fix the “low-fi grey blocks = poor UI” note; folders/docs fall back to tinted category tiles (the accent wheel as encoding, like syntax highlighting). The chunky bottom action bar of big single-purpose tiles is the InShot “object is the interface” multi-select pattern kept firmly in the thumb zone (Mobile_V5 thumb-first ergonomics); Delete alone carries error tint so destructive intent is never ambiguous. Soft depth and shadow (not flat outlines), a confident gradient storage-ring hero, filled cohesive icons and a lean Home · Recent · Cleaner bar deliver premium polish while staying native & trustworthy (dynamic color honoured, true dark first-class). Warmth and honesty — trust-first permission gate, friendly empty state, calm error (“nothing was changed”), and reversible delete with an Undo snackbar — are the BetterApp / warmth-is-a-feature and fast-&-honest principles. Every value maps to core-ui/theme tokens (design-system §6 dark family, §6.5 Home, §7 Browse, §8 multi-select) — nothing hard-coded, no second brand color, no 5th body size.
